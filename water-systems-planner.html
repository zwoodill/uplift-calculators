Implementation guide
DIY approach
  • 2–3" closed-cell spray foam direct to tank
  • Or rigid polyiso board box around the tank
  • Foil tape all seams and pipe stubs
  • Foil-faced bubble wrap as outer radiant barrier
  • No bare metal anywhere on pipes or fittings
Pre-insulated options
  • Target R-20+ factory insulation rating
  • Marine tanks often better insulated than RV units
  • Stainless custom tank + DIY foam is reliable
  • Most RV water heaters are poorly insulated — verify
  • Check: tank should be warm, not hot to the touch

"% batt/day" is standby loss as a share of the battery bank — heat bled 24/7 whether or not you shower. Upfront heat is one full inlet→storage fill; it isn't wasted (it returns as usable gallons), so the only true penalty for storing hotter is the standby column.

Where to land
Insulation cuts standby loss but adds to every outside dimension. Heat loss falls as 1/thickness (steep diminishing returns) while envelope volume climbs faster than linearly — past a point it's smarter to add water, not foam. ΔT tracks the storage temp above.

How to think about it
Composting toilet
No flush water, no black water. The open question is liquid handling — urine separation vs. a small leachate tank. Testing drives the choice; this sizes both.

Battery pack & loads
Pump sizing — usage → duty → pump → power
Your fixtures set the peak flow; static lift + fixture pressure + friction set the head. Together they are the duty point the pump must hit. The pump's efficiency at that duty sets its electrical draw, which flows into the DC balance above.
